My name is Haochang Luo — pronounced "Haw-Chung Loh", written as 罗昊昌.
Like my name, I study the sky.

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the City University of New York, City College. 🗽 My work focuses on the dynamics of large-scale convective systems and their interactions with other systems. Most of my targets are in the tropics and subtropics — the Indian Summer Monsoon Low Pressure Systems (MLPSs) and African Easterly Waves (AEWs). Recently I have been eyeballing the Intertropical Convergence Zone (ITCZ) and actively studying Machine Learning.

My research interests span precipitation, convection, fluid dynamics, thermodynamics, tropical waves, eddies and disturbances. My approaches include linear regressions, composites, empirical orthogonal function (EOF / PCA), maximum covariance analysis, and power spectrum analysis.

With 7+ years of Linux coding experience I am proficient in Python, NCL and Matlab. Daily packages include numpy, xarray, matplotlib, cartopy, pandas, pytorch, and self-developed algorithms. Overleaf is my primary writing tool.
